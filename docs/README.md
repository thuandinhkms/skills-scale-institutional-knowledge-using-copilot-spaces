# OctoAcme Project Management Docs

## Summary of OctoAcme Project Management Processes

OctoAcme uses a structured, iterative project management lifecycle designed to deliver customer value with clear ownership and continuous improvement.

**Initiation** validates business need, aligns stakeholders on goals and constraints, defines measurable success metrics, and establishes a clear go/no-go decision before work begins.

**Planning** converts approved goals into a prioritized backlog with acceptance criteria, effort estimates, dependency mapping, milestones, and a documented Definition of Done — creating a structured path from idea to delivery.

**Execution & Tracking** drives delivery through a consistent team rhythm: a project board with stages (Backlog → Ready → In Progress → In Review → QA → Done), daily standups, weekly delivery syncs, sprint demos, and rigorous pull request quality gates (CI checks, linked issues, required approvals).

**Risk Management & Communication** keeps stakeholders aligned and risks visible throughout delivery. Teams maintain a risk register (impact, likelihood, owner, mitigation, status) and follow a defined escalation path — from team triage to PM/Product Lead to sponsor — backed by regular stakeholder updates and a single source of truth for project status.

**Release & Deployment** standardizes pre-release readiness checks, deployment steps, post-deploy verification, and rollback procedures to ensure safe, consistent releases with minimal disruption.

**Retrospective & Continuous Improvement** closes each sprint, release, or incident with a structured retrospective that captures what worked, what didn't, and 2–3 owned action items — turning learnings into tracked, measurable process improvements.

Roles are clearly defined to maintain ownership and cross-functional alignment: **Developers** focus on implementation quality and technical risk; **Product Managers** own the problem statement, roadmap, and customer outcomes; **Project Managers** coordinate schedules, dependencies, and communications; **QA/Stakeholders** provide validation and approval.
